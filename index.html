I'm Blake Trebelhorn

A recent grad and full stack developer.

Learn a little bit about me and my interests:

Computer Programming GNU/Linux Building computers 3D Printing Boy Scouting Martial Arts Gaming
I started playing with technology in my middle school years when I first got an iPod touch. I wanted more out of the device, so I looked into how I could do that. I learned about Jailbreaking and went from there. My interest in technology continued to grow, so I sought out a formal education. Fast forward to today where I've graduated, and work as a full stack developer, and love my job.
If I had a choice, I'd use GNU/Linux for everything. I spent the last 2 years of college solely using Ubuntu on my laptop. I plan to continue exploring different distros, partially because I love setting up a new system.
One of my hobbies is building computers. I built my first computer when I was 17, and have built computers for my friends, family, and even University since. My current computer has an Intel i5 4690k overclocked to 4.5Ghz, 16GB of RAM, an Nvidia GTX 970, 2 monitors, and about 6TB of storage.
I first became interested in 3D printing my sophmore year of college. One of my professors printed a case for my Raspberry Pi. Since then I've printed countless things, and founded a club on campus, the Drake Innovators, that utilizes things like 3D printers, automated wood carving, and other things.
I initially started my Boy Scouts career in Kindergarten. I joined alongside my best friend, whom I still talk to every weekend. While in Cub Scouts, the lower section of Boy Scouts, I enjoyed learning many valuable skills. I moved up through the ranks of Cub Scouts each school year unti I reached Tenderfoot, the first rank of Boy Scouts. Boy Scouting offered much more that Cub Scouting did. Some of my favorite memories include a summer of biking which ended with a 50 mile ride in a single day. Another one is going on a weeklong camp each year with all of my friends, where we swam, climbed, cooked, and much more. One of the coolest things we did was camp in sub-zero temperatures for 24 hours, which included sleeping in an igloo we made ourselves. My greatest achievement in Scouting was attaining the rank of Eagle Scout, which is an honor only 4% of Boy Scouts receive. One of the requirements for an Eagle Scout is planning and carrying out a community service project. I designed and raised funds for a new dock at the local nature reserve. The original one was over 30 years old and was sinking into the ground. The dock I designed is larger and has a bench around ¾ of the structure.
As a child I was always interested in martial arts. For my 8th birthday my parents signed me up for a TaeKwonDo class at the rec center. I attended training two times a week with testing every few months to work my way up the ranks. I participated in tournaments where I fought, broke boards, and competed in forms; a sequence of moves in which you memorize. After 4 ½ years I achieved the rank of 1st dan blackbelt, the first of the 10 blackbelt ranks in TaeKwonDo.
If I'm not working, eating, or sleeping, chances are I'm probably on my computer gaming. It gives me a chance to talk to my friends who live far away, or escape reality and dive into a deep and well written story.
